The Marist Mission in East Asia

Rooted in the charism of St. Marcellin Champagnat, the Marist Brothers of East Asia have been transforming lives through integral, faith-based education for over a century — making Jesus known and loved through schools, mission communities, and pastoral service across the Asia-Pacific region.

Education as Mission

The Marist Brothers define education not merely as schooling, but as an act of faith — a lived encounter with the Gospel that forms the whole person in virtue, knowledge, and compassion.

Faith Formation

Every Marist school is a community of faith. Students encounter Christ through liturgy, service projects, religious instruction, and the lived witness of dedicated brothers and lay educators.

Regional Sectors

Three Sectors, One Mission

The East Asia Province is organized into three geographical sectors, each bringing the Marist charism to life within its unique cultural and ecclesial context.

China Sector Schools

Hong Kong · Malaysia · Singapore

The China Sector represents the Marist Brothers' century-long presence in the Chinese-speaking world and Southeast Asia. Rooted in Hong Kong since the early 20th century, the sector continues the Marist mission through quality Catholic schooling and pastoral service.

St. Francis Xavier's College

Located in Sham Shui Po, Hong Kong — one of the region's flagship Marist secondary schools serving thousands of students with rigorous Catholic education since 1931.

St. Francis Xavier's School

A primary feeder school in Hong Kong continuing the Marist tradition of nurturing young learners in a safe, faith-filled environment from their earliest years of schooling.

Malaysia & Singapore Communities

Marist Brothers serve in schools and communities across Penang and Kuala Lumpur (Malaysia) and Singapore, extending the charism into multicultural Southeast Asian societies.

Korea-Japan Sector

South Korea · Japan

Operating in two of Asia's most developed societies, the Korea-Japan Sector brings the Marist charism to minority Catholic communities through education, youth ministry, and social outreach — serving with humility in contexts where Christians are few but faith shines brightly.

Jechon Community, Korea

Based in Jecheon, North Chungcheong Province, the Korean Marist community accompanies youth through pastoral programs, school chaplaincy, and formation activities aligned with the Marist charism.

Kumamoto Community, Japan

In Kumamoto, Japan, Marist Brothers engage in school ministry and youth accompaniment, offering a Marian witness of faith in one of Asia's most pluralistic and technologically advanced societies.

Youth Ministry Focus

The sector prioritizes youth evangelization, retreat programs, and social service — reaching out to the marginalized and building vibrant young Church communities in Korea and Japan.

Philippine Sector

Luzon · Visayas · Mindanao

The Philippine Sector is the heartbeat of the East Asia Province — home to the Province Center, the largest concentration of Brothers, and an extensive educational network spanning the archipelago from Marikina in Metro Manila to General Santos City in Mindanao.

Marist School Marikina

Located in Metro Manila, Marist School Marikina offers quality Marist education from Grade School to Senior High School, including the cherished Marikina Evening High School serving 400+ underprivileged youth.

Notre Dame of Marbel University (NDMU)

A Marist-founded university in General Santos City, NDMU offers undergraduate and graduate programs in a deeply Catholic and Marian academic environment in Mindanao.

Notre Dame of Dadiangas University (NDDU)

Also in General Santos City and home to the Province Center, NDDU-IBED is one of the largest Marist-run educational institutions in the Philippines, serving thousands of students at all levels.

Marist Learning Centre (Myanmar Students)

Serving 460+ displaced Myanmar students, the Marist Learning Centre embodies the Brothers' option for the poor and their response to humanitarian crises through education.

Universal Mission

Marist Global Mission

Founded in 1817 in La Valla, France, by St. Marcellin Champagnat, the Congregation of the Marist Brothers was born with a single, urgent mission: to make Jesus known and loved among the poor and uneducated youth of rural France. That same fire now burns on every continent.

The Marist Brothers' global mission is animated by the Champagnat Global vision — a worldwide family committed to transforming lives, promoting justice, and educating the whole person. In East Asia, this mission finds expression in schools, non-formal education centers, formation houses, and community missions reaching some of the most vulnerable populations in the region.

Option for the Poor & Vulnerable

Marist Brothers preferentially serve the marginalized — from displaced refugees to impoverished rural communities — in fidelity to Champagnat's founding vision.

Evangelization through Education

Schools are not merely academic institutions but instruments of the New Evangelization — spaces where young people encounter the living Gospel of Jesus Christ.

Marian Spirituality

As Marists, the Brothers live and minister under Mary's maternal guidance — modeling the simplicity, humility, and loving care she showed in nurturing the infant Jesus.
